Small Business Consulting | Financial & Management

During the last two and a half years as a senior analyst for my Chicago-based consulting firm, I became an specialist in building complex Excel-based financial and operational models, an accounting expert, and an educator.

By spending my time between running clients' finance departments and sitting on their leadership team, teaching classes on accounting, finance, and Excel, and building complex financial reporting models and dashboards, I greatly improved my technical skills. However, more importantly, I developed an immensely greater EQ (emotional intelligence) - one of the most important business skills. Because of this role, I feel confident running high-pressure client and VC/PE meetings, managing multiple engagements, as well as pitching and defending my work.

Acting CFO and Financial Controller
Running a $50MM Finance Department

Working at a start-up, I was often thrown into the deep end. A staffing shake up less than six months into my role catapulted me into running an entire client account on my own. I accepted the challenge and it proved to be one of the most pivotal moments of my career. This entailed:

  • Running the entire finance and accounting department of this $50 million company.
  • Assisted in strategic decision making at the highest level by sitting on the leadership team weekly meetings and quarterly offsite where all critical decisions were made.
  • Participating in a board meeting to present financial results as well as act as facilitator (at just 24 years old).
  • Compile all financial reporting for banks, auditors, legal, and tax prep.
  • Build financial budgeting, cash flow, and acquisition models for analysis. This is where I began developing some of the complex financial models featured above.
  • Run point on a year-long wind-down to dissolve the business in order for the client to pivot.
  • Became well-versed in the business methodology and system known as "Traction."

One-on-One finance literacy weekly lessons with CEOs

Taught finance and accounting concepts in a weekly lesson with CEO clients. By engaging in one-on-one conversations directly with CEOs, I was forced to REALLY know my stuff. Many entrepreneurs and business owners don't know a lot about accounting, but they all recognize it would help them run their companies better. Because of this, I starting providing 15-30 minute weekly lessons on topics, like: double-entry accounting, cash vs. accrual, revenue recognition, differences between tax and book reporting, depreciation and amortization, bad debt allowances and write-offs, and many more topics.
